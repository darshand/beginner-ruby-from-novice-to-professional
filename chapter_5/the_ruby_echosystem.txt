Ruby
Named after gemstone (influenced by perl)

Developed in 1993
by Yukihiro Matsumoto (Matz) in Japan

“I wanted a language that was more powerful than Perl, and more object-oriented than Python. That’s why 
I decided to design my own language.”


Ruby alpha - December 1995
Ruby 1.0 - December 25, 1996

- Inflenced from perl, smalltalk
- There is more than one way to do it (TIMTOWTDI) is inherited from perl
- An official English language web site soon followed in late 1999 with the creation of ruby-lang.org (http://www.ruby-lang.org/)
- 37signals(a web design agency then) wanted a tool to run their business efficiently.
- contacted DHH (student from Copenhagen University, Denmark) to develop a tool
- July 2004 released framework to public 
- DHH become company partner
